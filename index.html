The Summer Slide

What is the summer slide? The summer slide refers to an achievement gap in school age children. While this gap affects all children, it is primarily seen in low-income and minority students in rural and urban areas (Smith 2012). The achievement gap is a one to three-month loss of academic progress during the summer months; this can be attributed to the lack of access to reading materials and summer learning activities (Smith 2012). Studies have shown that children lose academic progress, cumulatively over the summers, not only in reading but in math as well (Kerry 1998).

Current Solutions

There are many solutions to help with the summer slide. Some propose a full year school schedule, which in places has already been implemented (Kerry 1998). In some communities there are summer care/camp programs where children participate in fun learning activities as well as being provided meals. Almost all communities have a public library, which consistently promote reading and provide free summer programs and reading incentives. Public libraries are also promoting STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) programming, which addresses the acedemic gap in math by providing free access to new technology and hands-on application of science and engineering

Resources For Parents

There are many ways to make reading and math fun. Kids can read a book, a physical book, an electronic book, or even listen to an audiobook. There are numerous movies based on books that kids can read, especially a favorite like Harry Potter or A Wrinkle In Time. Almost every fairy tail movie originated with the book and it could be fun to discover the many different versions of the same story. Parents can encourage anyone their child sees daily to provide access to reading materials. It is important to share the awareness of the summer slide and how each person can help support the child's long term acedemic success!
